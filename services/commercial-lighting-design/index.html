Commercial Lighting Design

Design-led lighting for hospitality, retail, and commercial environments. Where aesthetics meet functionality — sophisticated schemes that transform spaces.

Lighting That Defines the Experience

Lighting is one of the most influential elements in any commercial space. It shapes atmosphere, guides behaviour, highlights architecture, and directly impacts how people feel within an environment. At Oleco, we approach commercial lighting design as a discipline that sits at the intersection of engineering and creative design — delivering schemes that are technically precise and aesthetically exceptional.

Our lighting design services are built for commercial clients who understand that their environment is a business asset. Hotels and restaurants where ambience drives repeat visits. Retail spaces where lighting influences purchasing decisions. Offices where well-designed illumination improves productivity and wellbeing. We design lighting that works for the business, not just the building.

Hospitality Lighting Design

The hospitality sector demands lighting that creates mood, supports service, and adapts to different uses throughout the day. A hotel lobby requires a different quality of light at 7am compared to 7pm. A restaurant needs intimate table lighting that coexists with functional kitchen and service area illumination. A bar needs energy and atmosphere without compromising comfort.

Our work on The Midland in Conwy is a testament to our approach to hospitality lighting. This characterful venue required a lighting scheme that enhanced its architectural features, created warmth and intimacy in dining areas, and provided flexible control for different service periods — all while meeting current safety and emergency lighting standards. The result is a space where the lighting feels effortless, despite the significant technical complexity behind it.

We design lighting for hotels, restaurants, bars, cafes, spas, event spaces, and boutique hospitality venues throughout Conwy, North Wales, and the wider UK.

Retail and Showroom Lighting

In retail environments, lighting is a commercial tool. The right lighting scheme can increase dwell time, improve product presentation, and create a brand experience that extends beyond the merchandise. We design retail lighting that balances ambient illumination with targeted accent and feature lighting, using colour temperature and intensity to complement your brand identity.

  • Display and merchandise lighting — precise spotlighting and track systems that showcase products at their best
  • Storefront and window lighting — high-impact schemes that draw attention and drive footfall
  • Brand-aligned colour temperature — warm, neutral, or cool tones selected to reinforce your brand positioning
  • Energy-efficient LED systems — reducing operational costs while maintaining exceptional light quality

Office and Corporate Lighting

Workplace lighting has a measurable impact on productivity, concentration, and staff wellbeing. Modern office lighting design goes far beyond rows of fluorescent panels. We design layered lighting schemes for corporate environments that incorporate circadian-responsive lighting, task lighting, collaborative area illumination, and architectural feature lighting that reflects your organisation’s identity.

Our office lighting designs comply with the Lighting Guide LG7 (office lighting) standards and are optimised for visual comfort, glare reduction, and energy performance.

Architectural and Accent Lighting

Architectural lighting reveals the character of a building — emphasising textures, defining spatial boundaries, and creating visual depth. We design schemes that use uplighting, downlighting, wall washing, grazing, and concealed linear LED to transform structural elements into visual features.

Accent lighting adds another layer of sophistication, drawing attention to artworks, displays, signage, or architectural details. The interplay between ambient, architectural, and accent lighting is what separates a well-lit space from a truly designed one.

LED Technology and Energy Efficiency

LED technology is at the core of our lighting design practice. Modern LED luminaires offer exceptional colour rendering, precise beam control, long operational life, and significant energy savings compared to legacy lighting technologies. We specify LED solutions from leading manufacturers, ensuring reliability, warranty coverage, and access to replacement components.

For clients looking to upgrade existing lighting, we offer comprehensive LED retrofit services. We assess your current lighting infrastructure, design a replacement scheme that improves both quality and efficiency, and manage the full installation with minimal disruption to your operations. For more detail on the commercial benefits, see our article on commercial LED lighting benefits.

Emergency Lighting Compliance

Emergency lighting is a legal requirement in all commercial premises and must comply with BS 5266. We integrate emergency lighting into every design, ensuring it is positioned correctly, tested to standard, and as unobtrusive as possible within the overall lighting scheme. Emergency lighting design is not an afterthought — it is part of the core design process.

Collaboration With Design Professionals

Many of our lighting projects involve close collaboration with architects, interior designers, and project managers. We welcome this collaborative approach and can engage at any stage — from concept development through to detailed design, specification, and installation. Our technical expertise complements the creative vision of the design team, ensuring the final result is both beautiful and buildable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Commercial Lighting Design FAQ

What types of commercial spaces do you design lighting for?
We design lighting for a wide range of commercial environments including hotels, restaurants, bars, cafes, retail stores, showrooms, offices, corporate headquarters, galleries, museums, and event spaces. Each project is tailored to the specific functional and aesthetic requirements of the space.
Do you work with architects and interior designers?
Yes, collaboration is central to our lighting design process. We regularly partner with architects, interior designers, and project managers to ensure the lighting scheme integrates seamlessly with the broader design vision. We can work from concept stage through to final installation.
What is the difference between architectural and accent lighting?
Architectural lighting refers to the primary illumination of a space, including general ambient light, task lighting, and structural feature lighting that defines the character of the environment. Accent lighting is used to highlight specific features, artworks, textures, or focal points within a space, adding depth and visual interest.
Do you handle emergency lighting compliance as part of the design?
Yes. Emergency lighting is a legal requirement in commercial premises, and we integrate it into every lighting design. Our emergency lighting systems comply with BS 5266 and are designed to be as unobtrusive as possible while meeting all safety requirements.
Can you retrofit LED lighting into existing commercial premises?
Absolutely. LED retrofitting is one of our most popular services. We assess your existing lighting infrastructure, design an LED replacement scheme that improves both quality and efficiency, and manage the full installation. Most clients see significant energy savings and improved light quality.
